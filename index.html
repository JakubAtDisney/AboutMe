👋 Howdy!

Thank you for taking a yonder at my Disney Goodbye Book and maybe even writing something inside. I created this website to share a little bit more about me, what I like to do, and how I got here.

From Europe to USA

I was born in Slovakia, which is a small country at the heart of Europe. At a very young age, my dad brought me to Illinois for what was supposed to be a summer vacation. That vacation quickly turned into me being enrolled in kindergarten, and from there, I've lived near the Chicago area for most of my life. I have visited my family back in Slovakia a few times, but I've always wondered what it would be like to live in Europe one day.

Growing Up

I remember watching a variety of animated movies and cartoons, which then became a passion for making my own videos for what feels like a million years now. I also love video games, so if you mention that you play some yourself, then we can chat for hours.

Not only that but I enjoy being creative by making art, creating cosplay, editing videos, playing the Ukulele, learning to code, or putting together my own animations. Fall and Winter are my favorite times of year, whereas Halloween is the only Holiday allowed to happen every day.

The Poster

Fast forward a few years: At my Community College, I always noticed a poster on the wall for the Disney College Program. Working at Walt Disney World sounded like a fun experience, so I finally asked about it and the next thing I knew, I was accepted to arrive for my program in January 2026.

The Struggle

So while still attending classes, I also worked overtime throughout the holidays at a retail job to save up enough money to pay for my school semesters, buy myself a laptop, plane tickets, new glasses, a Canister Filter for my turtle, proper work shoes, and to cover all of the Flamingo Crossing Dorm Fees. I came to Florida with under $100 to my name, which quickly went to my first week's rent and groceries.

The Disney College Program

Not only was this my first time in Florida, but also my first time living on my own and my first time visiting Walt Disney World too. So it’s been a huge change coming here, but I am happy to say that while it can be a very cheesy place, I completely see why people like it so much and visit so often. It's also very expensive, so I'm hoping I can bring my family here with the comp tickets we receive.

Those first few months of surviving on Oatmeal and Peanut Butter were a struggle, but I really enjoy the freedom of living away on my own and the peace of mind it has brought me… Plus, losing over 30+ pounds so far has been a nice bonus too. 10/10, highly recommend the “Broke Intern” diet.

Park Greeter

My initial role was as a Park Greeter at the Magic Kingdom. I didn’t even know what a Park Greeter was or anything about Walt Disney World aside from that it existed in Florida and had a Castle. But I learned many things working there and really enjoyed telling cheesy Dad jokes to the Guests who arrived. After 4 months of getting used to my routine and making friends, there were apparently too many College Program students placed as Park Greeters, so one by one, we were all slowly moved to different roles.

Adventureland

Eventually I was also picked to transfer roles from Park Greeter to Adventureland at The Magic Carpets of Aladdin, Walt Disney's Enchanted Tiki Room, and the Swiss Family Treehouse. When I heard this, it left me feeling incredibly frustrated and disheartened. Not only that, but the idea of operating an attraction where a single decision impacts guest safety is terrifying. One wrong move can completely alter someone's life, and that responsibility rests entirely in my hands.

Carpet Salesfolks

Despite those fears, I've genuinely come to enjoy my time here all thanks to just how nice and welcoming my fellow Carpet Salesfolks have been. I still make many mistakes, but your endless patience in answering my questions has helped me grow more confident every single day. While Park Greeter will always be my home, you have all made Adventureland the perfect vacation home.

I Graduated

Back in December, I bought a ticket to see the Music of Square Enix Symphony in Chicago, planning to also spend a few days visiting my family. Unbeknownst to me at the time, the symphony, my final semester, and my graduation ceremony all aligned perfectly with my trip home. So I decided on a whim that I might as well graduate and walk across the stage in person too.

I ended up flying from Florida to Chicago, took the Bus down to my campus, changed in the parking lot, and made it to the graduation by 30 minutes. I didn't get home until 2:00 AM that night... but I could finally say I graduated with a Multimedia Designer Certificate, a Graphics & Desktop Publishing Certificate, and an Associate of Arts degree. Maybe one day I'll be able to afford to continue my education even further.

The Future

I was originally scheduled to leave on August 13th, but didn't want to say goodbye, so I officially extended my program until January 14th, 2027. My ultimate goal is to either stay within the Disney Company or end up in Film Production, Video Game Development, Software Engineering, or Animation.

Even if that doesn't happen right away, I know this experience will open doors to incredible new paths I haven't even thought of yet. Either way, sharing this Walt Disney World journey with all of you has given me memories that will last a lifetime.

Let's Chat

Feel free to browse through the rest of my website. If you spot a favorite video game franchise or a movie and artist you recognize, let’s chat about it! I tend to open up slowly as I get to know people, so think of this site as my way of breaking the ice.

I'm also usually found attending the Learning Classes at Flamingo Crossing or playing games at the Community Center, so if you see me anywhere then stop by to say Howdy.

The Goodbye Book

I want to remember you not just as a Cast Member, but as a person. I'd love to read your story. Where are you from? How did you get here? What do you like to do? What do you hope the future will bring you?

I'm here until January 2027, so feel free to write whatever you’d like, and update it as your journey continues. I'm also on Instagram.
